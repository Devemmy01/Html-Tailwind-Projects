pure line rabbits

January 21st, 2022


What is a pure line rabbit? Our common Knowledge on rabbits will tell us that a pure line is a rabbit showing all distinguishing features of a particular rabbits breed. For example we could use a new Zealand white rabbit to sight an instance. By mere looks, a New zealand white rabbit has a white, short and silky fur, albino eyes and long venous ears.


This article will tell us more about pure lines and how you as a Farmer can actually breed pure line rabbits


A pure line according to ARBA (American Rabbit Breeders Association) is a rabbit produced down to a fourth (4th) Filial Genereation (offspring).


The above paragraph means, when you mate a New Zealand white doe with a New Zealand white buck (both having good traits) and she kindles the First Filial Genereation (offspring) then you take a doe from this first generation and mate them with their father. when she kindles, you take a doe from the daughter's offspring(granddaughter) and mate her with her grandfather (same dominant buck) to produce a Fourth(4th) and last offsprings can now be considered purelines.


However, this whole process takes a very long time to accomplish, a minimum of two(2) years with healthy and rapid growth and a maximum of 3-5 years. Sterility in bucks can also affect this process alongside diseases and helth challenges.


We can all breed pureline rabbits with patience and consistency. Breed a pureline today, go on a mind blowing experience.
